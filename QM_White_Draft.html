Stellar evolution

White Dwarf 白矮星

Equation

\[\begin{aligned} &\ce{^{1}H + ^{1}H -> ^{2}H + e^{+} + {\nu} + 1.44 \rm{MeV}}\\[2ex] &\ce{^{2}H + ^{1}H -> ^{3}H + {\gamma} + 5.49 \rm{MeV}}\\[2ex] &\ce{^{3}H + ^{3}H -> ^{4}H + ^{1}H + ^{1}H + 12.85 \rm{MeV}} \end{aligned}\] After all $\ce{^{1}H}$ burn out, these reaction stop. Gravitational contraction compress $\ce{He}$ until it begins start a new reaction. \[\begin{aligned} &\ce{^{4}He+ ^{4}He -> ^{8}Be+ {\gamma} -} \underset{{\rm barrier}}{9.6 \rm{keV}}\\[2ex] &\ce{^{8}Be + ^{4}He -> ^{12}C + {\gamma} + 7.4 \rm{MeV}} \end{aligned}\] and some other nuclear process.
This burning stops when the star consist primaryly "iron", "slilicon" an near by elements \[\text{graviational contraction}\underset{\text{balance}}\longleftrightarrow\text{degeneracy presssure}\]

Pressure

  • Graviationnal Pressure

    \[P_g = -\frac{1}{5}\left(\frac{4\pi}{3}\right)^{1/3} G \left(Nm_n\right)^2 V^{-4/3}\] ,which is toward the center of stellar.

    Fisrt, calculate the potential in a shell between $r$ and $r+dr$ \[dU_{g} = -G\frac{M_{0\sim r}\cdot M_{r \sim r+dr}}{r} = -G\frac{\left(\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 \rho \right)\left(4\pi r^2 dr \rho \right)}{r} = -\frac{(4\pi)^2}{3}G\rho r^4dr,\] where \[\begin{aligned} &N\text{ : number of nucleons in the stellar;}\\ &m_n\text{ : masses of those nucleons;}\\ &\rho \text{ : density of stellar;}\\ &V \text{ : volumn of stellar,}\\ \end{aligned}\] and \[\rho = \frac{Nm_N}{\frac{4}{3}\pi R^3 } \quad\Rightarrow \quad R = \left(\frac{3}{4\pi}Nm_N \frac{1}{\rho }\right)^{1/3} = \left(\frac{3}{4\pi}V\right)^{1/3}.\] Intergral $dU_g$, we might have the potential energy of stellar \[U_{g} = \int_{0}^{R} -\frac{(4\pi)^2}{3}G\rho r^4dr,\] then plug in the density, we have \[U_g = -\frac{3}{5}\left(\frac{4\pi}{3}\right)^{1/3} G \left(Nm_n\right)^2 V^{-1/3}.\] Since the pressure $P$ is taking the derivative of the energy with respect to the volume \[P_g = -\frac{\partial U_g}{\partial V} = \frac{3}{5}\left(\frac{4\pi}{3}\right)^{1/3} G \left(Nm_n\right)^2 \frac{\partial }{\partial V} \left(V^{-1/3}\right)\] , we may solve the gravitational pressure \[\boxed{P_g = -\frac{1}{5}\left(\frac{4\pi}{3}\right)^{1/3} G \left(Nm_n\right)^2 V^{-4/3}}\]


  • Degeneracy Pressure

    \[P_d = \frac{\hbar^2 \pi^3}{15m_e}\left(\frac{3Nq}{\pi}\right)^{5/3}V^{-5/3}\] , which is pointing outward.

    The degeneracy pressure is provided by electron ($e^{-}$). Here, assume

    \[ \left(\text{number of electrons } N_e\right) \approx \left(\text{number of protons }N_p\right) \approx \left(\frac{\text{number of nucleons }N}{2}\right)\]
    or we might using a factor $q=\frac{1}{2}$ to determine the ratio, the desity $n$ of $e^{-}$ is given by \[n=\frac{N_e}{V} = \frac{N/2}{V}=\frac{N}{V} q.\] The Fermi energy can be calculated by \[\begin{aligned} E_{\rm total} &=\int_{0}^{\varepsilon_{\!_{F}}} \varepsilon D(\epsilon)d\varepsilon =\frac{\sqrt{2}Vm_{e}^{3/2}}{\pi^2\hbar^3}\int_{0}^{\varepsilon_{\!_F}} \varepsilon^{3/2}{d\varepsilon} \\[2ex] &=\frac{\hbar^2\pi^{4/3}}{10m_e}\left(3N_e \right)^{5/3}V^{-2/3}. \end{aligned}\] Since the pressure $P$ is taking the derivative of the energy with respect to the volume \[\begin{aligned} P_d &= -\frac{\partial E_{\rm total}}{\partial V} = \frac{\hbar^2\pi^{4/3}}{10m_e}\left(3N_e \right)^{5/3}\frac{\partial}{\partial V} \left(V^{-2/3}\right) \\[2ex] & = \frac{\hbar^2 \pi^3}{15m_e}\left(\frac{3n}{\pi}\right)^{5/3} \end{aligned}\] , we may solve the degeneracy pressure of electron \[\boxed{P_d = \frac{\hbar^2 \pi^3}{15m_e}\left(\frac{3Nq}{\pi}\right)^{5/3}V^{-5/3}}\]


Radius

Solving for volumn $V$ making a balance between gravitational pressure and degeneracy pressure, \[\left|P_g\right| = \left|P_d\right|,\] then estimate the radius $R^*$ called "Critical Radius" \[R^*=\left(\frac{18\pi^2}{16}\right)^{1/3}q^{5/3}\, \frac{\hbar^2N^{-1/3}}{Gm_n^2m_e}\]

Let $\left|P_g\right| = \left|P_d\right|$, i.e. \[\frac{1}{5}\left(\frac{4\pi}{3}\right)^{1/3} G \left(Nm_n\right)^2 V^{-4/3} = \frac{\hbar^2 \pi^3}{15m_e}\left(\frac{3Nq}{\pi}\right)^{5/3}V^{-5/3}\] then \[\begin{aligned} V^{-1/3} &= \frac{\frac{1}{5}\left(\frac{4\pi}{3}\right)^{1/3} G \left(Nm_n\right)^2}{\frac{\hbar^2 \pi^3}{15m_e}\left(\frac{3Nq}{\pi}\right)^{5/3}} \\[2ex] & = \left(\frac{4\pi}{3}\right)^{1/3} G\left(Nm_n\right)^2 \frac{3m_e}{\hbar^2\pi^3}\left(\frac{\pi}{3Nq}\right)^{5/3} \end{aligned}\] solving that the balance volumn \[V^{-1/3} = \left(\frac{4\pi}{3}\right)^{1/3} G\left(Nm_n\right)^2\frac{3m_e}{\hbar^2\pi^{3}}\left(\frac{\pi}{3Nq}\right)^{5/3}.\] Suppose it is a sphere \[V = \frac{4}{3}\pi R^3 \quad\Rightarrow\quad R = \left(\frac{3}{4\pi}\right)^{1/3} V^{1/3},\] plugging \[\begin{aligned} R^* &= \left(\frac{3}{4\pi}\right)^{1/3} \left(\left(\frac{4\pi}{3}\right)^{1/3} G\left(Nm_n\right)^2\frac{3m_e}{\hbar^2\pi^{3}}\left(\frac{\pi}{3Nq}\right)^{5/3}\right)^{-1} \\[2ex] &= \left(\frac{3}{4\pi}\right)^{2/3}\frac{\hbar^2\pi^{3}}{G\left(Nm_n\right)^23m_e}\left(\frac{3Nq}{\pi}\right)^{5/3} \\[2ex] & = \left(\frac{9}{16\pi^2}\right)^{1/3}\frac{\pi^{3} 3^{5/3} }{3\pi^{5/3}}\, q^{5/3}\,\frac{\hbar^2 N^{5/3}}{G\left(Nm_n\right)^2m_e} \\[2ex] & = \left(\frac{81}{16\pi^2}\right)^{1/3}\pi^{4/3}\, q^{5/3}\,\frac{\hbar^2 N^{-1/3}}{Gm_n^2m_e} \\[2ex] & = \left(\frac{81\pi^2}{16}\right)^{1/3}\, q^{5/3}\,\frac{\hbar^2 N^{-1/3}}{Gm_n^2m_e}. \end{aligned}\] Solving the Critical Radius \[R^* = \left(\frac{81\pi^2}{16}\right)^{1/3}q^{5/3}\,\frac{\hbar^2 N^{-1/3}}{Gm_n^2m_e}.\]
